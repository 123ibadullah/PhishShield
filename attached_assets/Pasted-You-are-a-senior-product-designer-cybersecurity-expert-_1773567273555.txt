You are a senior product designer, cybersecurity expert, and full-stack engineer.

Upgrade and refine the project "PhishShield AI" into a highly polished, natural, human-designed cybersecurity product.

GOAL:
The application must NOT look AI-generated. It should feel like a real startup-grade product built by experienced engineers and designers.

---

1. OVERALL FEEL:
- Make the UI clean, minimal, and intentional
- Avoid generic or repetitive layouts
- Ensure everything feels thoughtfully placed (spacing, alignment, hierarchy)
- The product should feel calm, professional, and trustworthy

---

2. BRANDING:
- Product name: "PhishShield AI"
- Tagline: "Detect. Explain. Protect."
- Add a subtle shield-style icon or logo
- Add a small line: "Built for Indian users 🇮🇳"
- Keep branding minimal but strong

---

3. COLOR SYSTEM:
- Primary: Dark blue (trust, cybersecurity)
- Secondary:
  - Green (safe)
  - Yellow (warning)
  - Red (phishing/danger)
- Background: soft light or dark neutral tone
- Avoid overly bright or flashy colors

---

4. LAYOUT STRUCTURE:
- Centered layout with proper margins
- Sections:
  1. Header (branding)
  2. Input section
  3. Result section
- Use card-based design with soft shadows and rounded corners
- Maintain clear visual hierarchy

---

5. INPUT SECTION:
- Large clean textarea with placeholder:
  "Paste suspicious email content here..."
- Add a well-designed “Scan Email” button
- Add subtle hover and click animations
- Keep it simple and distraction-free

---

6. LOADING EXPERIENCE:
- Add a short “Scanning…” state (0.5–1 sec)
- Use smooth transition before showing results
- Avoid instant jump — make it feel alive

---

7. RESULT SECTION (VERY IMPORTANT):
- Display result inside a premium card
- Show:
  - Large label (Safe / Suspicious / Phishing)
  - Risk score (prominent)
  - Confidence percentage
- Use color-coded borders or accents (not full harsh backgrounds)

---

8. EXPLANATION (HUMAN LANGUAGE):
Replace robotic text with natural explanations.

Examples:
- Instead of: "Suspicious patterns detected"
- Use: "This email is trying to create urgency to pressure you"

- Instead of: "URL anomaly"
- Use: "The link looks similar to a trusted site but may be fake"

Make all explanations:
- simple
- human
- easy to understand

---

9. SUSPICIOUS CONTENT HIGHLIGHTING:
- Highlight risky words in red inside the email
- Keep it subtle and readable (not too aggressive)
- Make it visually helpful, not overwhelming

---

10. LINK ANALYSIS SECTION:
- Show detected links in a separate clean section
- Display:
  - status (safe/suspicious)
  - reason (lookalike domain, keyword-based risk)

---

11. MICRO-INTERACTIONS:
- Button hover effects
- Smooth fade-in for results
- Slight transitions between states
- Everything should feel smooth and responsive

---

12. HUMAN TOUCH:
- Avoid perfect robotic sentences
- Use slightly natural phrasing
- Add small practical lines like:
  - "Take a moment before clicking any link"
  - "Scammers often create urgency to trick users"

---

13. REAL-WORLD TRUST SIGNALS:
- Add tips:
  - "Banks never ask for OTP via email"
  - "Verify sender before taking action"
- Mention Indian context (SBI, UPI scams)

---

14. REMOVE AI-GENERATED FEEL:
- Avoid repetitive UI blocks
- Avoid overuse of bullet lists
- Ensure spacing and typography feel custom
- Keep design subtle and mature

---

15. FINAL EXPECTATION:
The result should feel like:
- a real cybersecurity startup product
- built for real users
- demo-ready for a national-level hackathon

Focus on clarity, trust, usability, and real-world feel.
